ForfarFive

January 2017–Ongoing

I've been passionate about football from a very young age, my mum brought me my first Norwich City shirt in 1991 and I attended my first ever match a year later. I have developed a big interest in sports photography, football in particular, and decided to start a project of my own. I love travelling, and most places I visit I try squeeze in a football match or 2. The view of the stadium from the train, seeing supporters drinking before a game, wearing their colours, walking down back streets to the ground, reading the matchday programme, these are moments I cherish more than the game itself. This project will be an ongoing journey into my own career as a football photographer, a showcase of supporter portraits, action shots and stories surrounding individuals.

What's in the name?

There's one job I would love to do, and James Alexander Gordon did it perfectly. The iconic voice in British broadcasting read the Saturday afternoon classified football results for the BBC, and despite East Fife never losing at home to Forfar in a 9 goal thriller, its story will always live on.
